Most families don't think about funeral planning until they're in the middle of a crisis. A phone call comes. Someone is gone. And suddenly, within 24 to 72 hours, you're making thousands of dollars worth of decisions while you're grieving.

The Conversation Your Family Needs to Have

Nobody wants to sit around the dinner table talking about death. But here's the reframe: it's not a conversation about death. It's a conversation about love.

It's saying to the people you care about: "I want to make sure that when the day comes, you're not scrambling. You're grieving. And that's all you should have to do."

These are the things worth talking about now:

  • Do you want a traditional funeral service, a celebration of life, or something else?
  • What songs matter to you?
  • Is there a scripture, a poem, or a quote that represents your life?
  • Who do you want to speak?
  • What does a meaningful goodbye look like to you?

None of this costs anything to discuss. But having it settled is worth everything to the people you leave behind.

The Program Is the One Thing People Keep

Flowers fade. Food gets eaten. But a funeral program gets kept. It gets tucked into Bibles. It gets framed. It gets pulled out years later when someone needs to remember a face, a name, a date.

"No family should have to say goodbye without a program. That's why FuneralReady exists."

When families come to me in crisis, the first thing I tell them is: let's take five minutes and do this right. Because the program you hold during the service is the one you'll hold twenty years from now.
